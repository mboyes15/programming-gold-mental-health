Sleep

Young people getting less than their required sleep are more likely to develop many health problems such as: type 2 diabetes, poor mental health, and injuries.

Information is from Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Sleep and Health. Avaiable at cdc.com/healthyschools accessed at (22/10/22)

How to get better sleep?

  1. Have a consistent Bed-time and Wake-up time
  2. No phones (or blue light) 30mins before you go to bed
  3. Make sure your room is completely dark, could try black out blinds for example

Social

Social media means that teenagers never have a break from the social pressures of school. This makes having strong and healthy mental health harder to maintain.

Also online ads and influencers directly change the malleable minds of young adults with fashion trends, consumer habits and lifestyle habits. Making it hard to switch off after a long day

The online world gives a false sense of perfection often causing us to feel like we aren't succeding enough if we aren't perfect. For example, students can feel bad about their study habits, their beauty or the materials in their possession not being enough.

Advantages of social media

  • Can give support wherever people need it
  • The large amount of resources online mean that people can teach themselves many different skills
  • Can give an outlet from the stresses of real life
